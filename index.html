About Me

I grew up thinking I'd be an engineer, which progressed to wanting to engineer medical devices. I went on to get a degree in biomedical engineering and work in the field of medical device R&D for 6 years. Over those 6 years I realized I was wrong growing up. The life of an engineer who works to develop medical devices was not for me. At the age of 29 and after 6 years of giving engineering a try I decided to quit my job and pursue a career in web development. I am currently in a 3-month full-time full stack web development bootcamp hoping to find the career that is right for me.

When I'm not working diligently to pursue a new career I enjoy Curling, hanging out with my wife and dog, exploring the Twin Cities, and learning how to make physical things. I have hobby aspirations to learn more about saws, routers, nail guns, CNC's, and all other sorts of tools that could help me make interesting physical objects, or cause me to loose appendages. I hope to be able to meld together my knowledge of code and building physical things to make fun and strange projects.
